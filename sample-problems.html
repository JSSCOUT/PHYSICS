📝 Sample Problems

Five fully worked examples — follow each step to master the problem-solving process.

Step-by-Step Solutions

Each problem shows: Given → Find → Formula → Solution → Answer.

Problem 1 — Ohm's Law

Situation: A resistor of 10 Ω has a current of 2 A flowing through it. Find the voltage across the resistor.
Given: Resistance R = 10 Ω, Current I = 2 A
Find: Voltage V = ?
Formula: V = I × R
Solution: V = 2 A × 10 Ω = 20 V
✅ Answer: V = 20 V

Problem 2 — Electric Power

Situation: An electrical device operates at 120 V and draws a current of 5 A. Find the power consumed.
Given: Voltage V = 120 V, Current I = 5 A
Find: Power P = ?
Formula: P = V × I
Solution: P = 120 V × 5 A = 600 W
✅ Answer: P = 600 W

Problem 3 — Coulomb's Law

Situation: Two point charges q₁ = 2×10⁻⁶ C and q₂ = 3×10⁻⁶ C are separated by a distance of r = 0.05 m. Find the electrostatic force between them.
Given: q₁ = 2×10⁻⁶ C, q₂ = 3×10⁻⁶ C, r = 0.05 m, k = 9.0×10⁹ N·m²/C²
Find: Force F = ?
Formula: F = k · q₁ · q₂ / r²
Solution: F = (9.0×10⁹) × (2×10⁻⁶) × (3×10⁻⁶) / (0.05)²
      = (9.0×10⁹ × 6×10⁻¹²) / 0.0025
      = 0.05394 / 0.0025
      = 21.576 N
✅ Answer: F ≈ 21.576 N (repulsive, since both charges are positive)

Problem 4 — Electric Field

Situation: A point charge Q = 5×10⁻⁶ C is located at the origin. Find the electric field strength at a distance r = 0.1 m from the charge.
Given: Q = 5×10⁻⁶ C, r = 0.1 m, k = 9.0×10⁹ N·m²/C²
Find: Electric Field E = ?
Formula: E = k · Q / r²
Solution: E = (9.0×10⁹) × (5×10⁻⁶) / (0.1)²
      = 44,950 / 0.01
      = 4,495,000 N/C
✅ Answer: E = 4,495,000 N/C = 4.495×10⁶ N/C

Problem 5 — Magnetic Force on a Wire

Situation: A wire of length L = 2 m carries a current of I = 3 A and is placed in a magnetic field B = 0.5 T. The wire is perpendicular to the field (θ = 90°). Find the magnetic force on the wire.
Given: B = 0.5 T, I = 3 A, L = 2 m, θ = 90°
Find: Force F = ?
Formula: F = B · I · L · sin θ
Solution: F = 0.5 T × 3 A × 2 m × sin(90°)
      = 0.5 × 3 × 2 × 1
      = 3 N
✅ Answer: F = 3 N
